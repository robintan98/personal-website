Machine Learning Research

Toward Improving Electrocardiogram (ECG) Biometric Veriﬁcation using Mobile Sensors: A Two-Stage Classiﬁer Approach

Portland State University | 2015 - 2016

I developed a novel biometric identification system by applying random forests and discrete wavelet transform to electrocardiogram data. My proposed two-stage classification algorithm demonstrates higher accuracy than existing single-stage classifiers and has the distinct advantage of using real mobile ECG sensor data.

Notable Honors: Publication in Sensors Journal (2017) and IEEE ICMLA Conference (2016), Google Science Fair Regional Finalist (Top 100 Global), 1st Place in Computer Science and Robotics Category - Northwest Science Expo, Intel Excellence in Computer Science Award.


Wavelet-Coupled Machine Learning Methods for Drought Forecast Utilizing Hybrid Meteorological and Remotely-Sensed Data

Portland State University | 2014 - 2015

I developed a statistical drought early warning system using artificial neural networks, support vector machines, and discrete wavelet transform with geological and satellite data. My drought forecasting system reliably and effectively predicts short-term droughts in the Central Valley region in California.

Notable Honors: Publication in DMIN Conference (2015), 2nd Place in Environmental Science Category, Gresham-Barlow Science Expo, Tom Owen Award for Excellence in Statistics, Outstanding Project Awards from U.S. Air Force and American Meteorological Society.


Exploring Biomolecular Computing Utilizing Reservoir Computing with Artificial Chemistry to Improve Pathogen Detection

Portland State University | Summer 2017

I explored the synergy of artificial chemistry and reservoir computing to improve classification problems in pathology as part of a summer AI research internship at Portland State University with Professor Christof Teuscher. Through this internship, I programmed my own classificication system by using an Abstract Rewriting System on Multisets and an Echo State Network.

Notable Honors: Project recognized and sponsored by the National Science Foundation.


Improving and Generalizing Image Recognition Algorithm Situate to Handshake Detection

Portland State University | Summer 2016

I improved upon the image recognition Situate by applying convolutional neural networks to handshake detection as part of a summer AI research internship at Portland State University with Professor Melanie Mitchell. I received first-hand experience programming multiple machine learning methods including principal component analysis, K-Means clustering, and perceptrons.

Notable Honors: Project recognized and sponsored by the National Science Foundation.




Industry

Analyzing Radio.com User Listening Behavior to Allow Entercom to Make More Informed Product and Marketing Decisions

Entercom/CBS Radio | Spring 2019

As a Wharton Analytics Fellow Project Analyst for Entercom/CBS Radio, I conducted retention analysis to determine key consumer listening habits and return on investment analysis of Entercom's marketing campaigns for the company's flagship internet broadcast platform, Radio.com, using Python. Along with other Wharton undergraduate and MBA data scientists, I presented our findings to Entercom executives, for which they praised our team for providing valuable insights and analytics to redefine their marketing strategy.
